Switching it up: modding my Nintendo Switch

A few weeks ago I came across some images of the Nintendo Switch dev kit online. Whilst dev kits aren't that interesting to the average gamer, one thing struck me about the Switch dev kit: the striking black colour scheme for the joy-cons. If you ask me, this looks way nicer than any other joy-cons that nintendo will sell you. I knew I had to have it.

I wasn't about to go and buy a dev kit (even though they're quite reasonably priced). So, I started looking for alternatives. The first thing that came across my mind was a skin from a company such as dbrand, but this was outside of my budget, and I fancied a more 'permanent' solution than a skin.

Eventually I settled on purchasing a set of replacement joy-con housings from Amazon. There's some really cool designs available, including ones based on NES and SNES controllers, and this gorgeous wave design. But, since I'm boring, I just went for plain black. I didn't need to re-house the main console either, since it's black already.

Let's get modding!

Next, I had to disassemble the controllers. This ain't easy. Nintendo has crammed a bunch of tech into the joy-cons, and internally they're a mess of tiny wires and ribbon cables. I'm no stranger to working with electronics, but this was brain surgery compared to anything I've done before.
